22nd of June Week 1

His company recently recieved new funding from investors and we discussed how his businesses is looking for developers to push his app to market. We agreed to keep in touch to see if I can assist with any development in the future. This has really highlighted to me the value of networking- going from this, I will dedicate some time next week to updating my proffesional profiles (i.e LinkedIn). I may also consider creating a portfolio website for myself: where potential employers or clients may view may work.

One final note for this week: I thrive under accountability and specifically pressure. After my meeting with Densie this week, I have found that my motivation has been seriously bolstered, I'm excited for this level of energy to continue as I learn and grow as a developer, and grow my own business, and for that I am very thankful for.

1st of July Week 2

This last week there has been a big focus on networking, as a result of the success of the networking activities of the prior. I remotely the Deploy- Power your business seminar. There was an opportunity for online networking on a group forum, however, this was nowhere near as effective as my one-to-one the week prior. Despite this, the seminar still provided very beneficial information in the seminars, such as growth strategies for startups or Social Media Marketing for Organic Engagement. This seminar was exceptionally useful as it provided primarily advice targeted at those with business models similar to the one I wish to develop (Saas). This experience has also taught me something about the way I develop professionally: being thrown into the deep end of professional situations that I may not be comfortable in, results in rapid professional skill growth. I had also spent some time updating and improving some elements of my LinkednIn profile (as mentioned the previous week) so that I can leverage this tool better when networking.
During a discussion during my weekly evening software developer meeting, the importance of a well maintained and polished GitHub account is for employers and other developers you may be working with. Following this discussion, I will be ensuring that my work is well documented on the GitHub platform to ensure that I appear professional to any interested persons.

When working under time scales or any under external pressure, my mind looks to ways to achieve as much as possible in as little time. One way I have found to achieve this is time chunking with Pomodoro timing. This means that I set goals for myself for each segment of time that I must complete within that time. This first achieves the effect of accountability for the work I complete and also achieves the impact of gratification for when I achieve these goals, which helps with further motivation. I think to expand the scope of this so far successful technique, I would like to set daily, weekly and monthly goals for myself, and hold myself to account for these.

11th of July Week 3

This last week has had me begin to take the exciting yet terrifying first steps towards starting my business. Speaking with my new business mentor has spiralled out to a wide variety of new opportunities and has sparked a wealth of opportunities that have flooded my to-do lists. This has also meant that this week has brought away focus from my technical work as I focussed on the business side more. This is a balance that I will need to learn to adapt to, especially being the sole individual responsible for the operation of the business and the technical operation of it. Going forward I will be 'tagging' tasks in my system to differentiate between my technical and business tasks. Last week I set up my Daily, Weekly and Monthly task system- this has been a success in the way that it has allowed me to set achievable and realistic goals for myself that translate into an ability to organise each of my days and weeks around these goals. I have been looking into alternative methods such as the Get Things Done method to further optimise the way I organise my work.

I have also been spending lots of time this last week looking at potential sources of funding and support for my business, in order to apply for any of these I must be able to present my business case, so I will be putting a high priority effort towards formulating my business plan and strategy so that I can move forward with these further funding opportunities. In order to further my professional development I have signed up for an in-person networking event at BRITE, I am also hoping to sign up for their 12-month PROFITNET program. By accessing all of this business support I hope to fill in the gaps of my business knowledge with external support and increase my likelihood of success by surrounding myself with peers that will push me to the best I can be. Going forward I really would like to push as much as I can on my own technical work and then return to the business over the weekend.
